How FraudGod uses adversarial intelligence from federal cases to catch synthetic identities that traditional systems miss.

The $6 Billion Problem

Synthetic identity fraud is the fastest-growing type of financial crime in the United States. Unlike traditional identity theft, where criminals steal an existing person's information, synthetic fraud involves creating entirely new "people" using a combination of real and fabricated data.

The typical synthetic identity starts with a real Social Security Number‚Äîoften belonging to a child, elderly person, or recent immigrant‚Äîcombined with a fake name, fabricated address history, and manufactured credit profile. These identities are carefully "warmed" over 12-24 months before executing a coordinated bust-out across multiple institutions.

Why Traditional Detection Fails

Rule-based fraud systems were designed for a different era. They catch obvious red flags‚Äî mismatched SSNs, blacklisted addresses, known fraud rings. But synthetic identities are designed from the ground up to pass these checks.

A well-constructed synthetic has:

  • A valid SSN that belongs to someone who isn't actively using credit
  • Consistent address history built over months
  • Credit scores artificially inflated through authorized user tradelines
  • Email, phone, and device fingerprints that appear legitimate

By the time a synthetic applies for your credit product, they've already beaten traditional verification. The question isn't whether they're "real"‚Äîit's whether their behavior patterns match known fraud schemes.

Real Detection Patterns from Federal Cases

Every pattern in FraudGod maps to documented fraud techniques. Here are examples from actual prosecutions:

Credit Privacy Number (CPN) Scheme
DOJ 2023

A fraud ring sold "Credit Privacy Numbers" to consumers, claiming they were legal alternatives to SSNs. The CPNs were actually SSNs stolen from deceased individuals, children, and incarcerated persons. Defendants helped buyers build credit profiles using these numbers.

Detection: CPNs often use SSN ranges never issued by SSA, including the 9XX series (reserved for ITINs), 000 and 666 area codes, and ranges overlapping with EINs (80-95).

Authorized User Tradeline Mill
DOJ 2022

Defendants operated a "tradeline renting" service where they added synthetic identities as authorized users on aged credit card accounts. Each synthetic was added to 6-10 tradelines, instantly inheriting years of positive payment history and boosting scores by 100+ points.

Detection: Legitimate consumers rarely have more than 2-3 AU accounts. 6+ AU accounts on a thin credit file is a strong synthetic indicator, especially combined with impossible credit velocity (high score + young file).

Coordinated Bust-Out Operation
DOJ 2021

A fraud network maintained 50+ synthetic identities, each "warmed" for 18 months with legitimate payment activity. On a coordinated date, all synthetics applied for credit increases, maxed out available credit, and disappeared. Total losses exceeded $1.2 million across 12 financial institutions.

Detection: Application velocity analysis catches coordinated attacks. Multiple applications from the same device fingerprint, datacenter IP, or within suspiciously short timeframes indicate fraud farm activity.

Ghost Identity Construction
CFPB 2023

Fraudsters created identities with no verifiable history‚Äîno previous addresses, no utility records, no public records. These "ghost" identities were seeded into data aggregators through strategic utility payments and rewards program signups before applying for credit.

Detection: Real consumers leave data trails. An applicant with zero address history, recently created email, and no utility footprint matches identity fabrication patterns.
